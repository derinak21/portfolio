Project information

  • Category: Robotics and Sensor Based Systems
  • Project date: June 2022
  • Skills: Circuit Design

Details

The collaborative project involved the development of a rover with the capability to identify rocks through the processing and analysis of diverse signals, fields, and electromagnetic waves. The rover utilized magnetic field, infra-red waves, and radio wave sensors to effectively distinguish rocks while on the move. Meticulous sensor selection and the implementation of a specialized antenna for radio wave processing ensured optimal performance. The team designed circuits that received, amplified, and inverted the sensor outputs as required for transmission to the Arduino. To ensure accuracy and precision, noise reduction was achieved through the integration of filters into the circuits. The rover's body, designed through 3D modeling and laser cutting, housed the Arduino, PCB, battery pack, and circuits, while an arm securely held the sensors in close proximity to the rocks. Through a remote app, the rover's speed and direction were controlled during long-distance travel. The app processed information sent from the Arduino and effectively distinguished different rock types.
