MazeBot Mapper: Autonomous Maze Mapping Rover

  2. MazeBot Mapper: Autonomous Maze Mapping Rover

Project information

  • Category: Robotics and Autonomous Systems
  • Project date: June 2023
  • Skills: ESP32, IMU, Control Systems, C++
  • Github Repo: Link

Details

A fully integrated self-balancing rover was developed with the ability to autonomously navigate and accurately map mazes, presenting the mapped maze on a dedicated website. The rover effectively processes data from computer vision, ultrasonic sensors, and an inertial measurement unit (IMU) sensor to facilitate its movement. Powered by an ESP32 processor, the rover employs dead reckoning and triangulation, guided by three distinct colored beacons, to determine its position within the maze. Utilizing an algorithm, the rover expertly follows the walls on its right as it efficiently maps the maze during its exploration. Through a seamless WIFI connection facilitated by the ESP32, the rover transmits its real-time position data to the website. Both the rover's location and the maze layout are displayed. To ensure balance on two wheels, a PID controller was implemented, leveraging data from the IMU sensor. This algorithm intelligently utilizes the data gathered from the computer vision and sensors, further enhancing the rover's mapping capabilities.
